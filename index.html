About Me

I am currently working at the Alberta Gaming and Liquor Comission (AGLC) as a Data Scientist analyzing the impact of different factors on gaming revenue such as the impact of economy. I also work on different business cases such as minizing costs on the installment of new slots. Some major side projects include developing tools to optimize sales.

I graduated from the the University of British Columbia (UBC) with a M.Sc. in Statistics, and have previously completed a B.Sc. degree in Statistics at UBC. During my M.Sc. program, I worked as a research assistant in the electrical and computer engineering department where I applied machine learning techniques to wireless communication systems to improve predictive performance of radio signal waveform detection. I also worked at Environment Canada as a co-op student where I managed air quality data and built models and frameworks to forecast air quality.
